K1 Kickboxing

K1 kickboxing is one of the most intense striking martial arts in the world, combining techniques from Muay Thai, Karate, and Western Boxing into a fast-paced, explosive combat sport.

Fighters use punches, kicks, and knee strikes to score points or achieve knockouts. Unlike Muay Thai, elbows are not allowed and clinching is limited, keeping the action focused on dynamic striking exchanges.

Matches consist of three 3-minute rounds. Victory can be achieved by knockout, technical knockout (3 knockdowns in one round), or judges' decision based on effective strikes, damage, and aggression.

K1 has grown into a global phenomenon with major international competitions including European Championships, World Cups, and World Championships organized by federations like WAKO (World Association of Kickboxing Organizations).
